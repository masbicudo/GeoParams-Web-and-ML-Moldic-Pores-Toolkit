About data and anonymity

During the research experiment, participants interacted with petrographic images by selecting segmentation parameters. Participants could request anonymized participation during data collection.

The public release does not include participant names, email addresses, free-text feedback, or any mapping between a person and their interactions. The published data retain only the interaction records needed to reproduce the analyses, such as selected parameter-space points and related image measurements.

Random folder identifiers may still be used internally to organize related files. They are technical storage identifiers and are not linked to participant identities in the public release.

Researchers may use this software to conduct a new experiment with their own participants. Any collection and handling of identifiable data in a new study is the responsibility of that study's organizers.
